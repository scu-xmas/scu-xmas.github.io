It’s been an extraordinary year. We’ve been inspired by tales of adaptation, tales of excellence, of the world getting smaller, ideas getting bigger, people defying expectations and shining a light on what matters. As this truly weird year draws to a close, here are 20 stories from 2020 at Southern Cross University to make you think, smile and wonder what next year holds in store.

Scroll down for more

We looked on the bright side

Southern Cross scientist Dr Dan Harrison hooked up a modified snow blower and blew vaporised water into the clouds above the Great Barrier Reef. It sounds like science fiction, but cloud brightening could help protect the reef from mass bleaching events.

Keep scrolling!

Fossil dating led to rethink of human evolution

Meanwhile, our very own fossil dating expert Associate Professor Renaud Joannes-Boyau was part of a team that proved there were several species of human living in Africa 300,000 years ago. They’ve all disappeared – except us.

Keep scrolling!

New Health Sciences building got first-year cohort through the doors

Our beautiful new multi-million-dollar Health Sciences building at Coffs Harbour signalled a new era of health education in this coastal paradise, with space for everything from exercise science and nursing to occupational therapy.

Keep scrolling!

COVID struck and everything moved online

It was all online but it wasn’t boring! Music students produced collaborative medieval chanting, lecturers delivered lectures on fish sampling from the field, social work students completed their training online and zoom became our new BFF.

Keep scrolling!

We were starry-eyed

The latest round of the Good Universities Guide saw a number of 5-star rankings awarded to Southern Cross. Notably, we picked up number 1 in Australia for overall experience in agriculture, environmental sciences, engineering and technology.

Keep scrolling!

Regenerative Agriculture postgrad course launched

In a year that underlined the importance of a global community working together, our Australia-first regenerative agriculture program launched a new course for postgraduate students, aimed at repairing the damage wrought by many conventional agricultural practices.

Keep scrolling!

The outback inspired and delighted

Southern Cross visual arts graduate and plein air painter Emily Imeson was awarded the 2020 Brett Whiteley Travelling Artists Scholarship. Along with $10,000 prize money she was also granted a two-week residency and mentorship by leading Australian artists such as Ben Quilty.

Keep scrolling!

Tourists were recruited to save the reef

Our scientists worked with tourists in the Whitsundays to harvest and nurture coral larvae in a scaled-up trial of Coral IVF, a world-first regeneration technique to repair the reef, pioneered by our very own Professor Peter Harrison.

Keep scrolling!

Biomed innovation made at SCU
wowed NASA

RapidRepair advanced wound technology, developed by Southern Cross University’s Dr Rosemary Craig and Dr Nedeljka Rosic with business consultant Gerard Criss, won the global NASA iTech Finals. Their innovative dressing can replace stitches and reduce healing time from weeks to days.

Keep scrolling!

Anyone say COVID…innovation?

Southern Cross launched the first of three R&D Challenges to support businesses developing a product or solution that directly addresses the impact of COVID-19, destined for market within 12 months. The prize pool was half a million dollars.

Keep scrolling!

We kept the conversation going

Despite being unable to gather in person this year, we found ways to connect. The online Thursday Night Live! sessions included an all-Indigenous online panel for NAIDOC week, marine scientists, historians and an all-Deaf panel, attracting hundreds of live listeners and many more to the recorded sessions.

Keep scrolling!

In the Year of the Nurse and Midwife, superhero nurse shone

Nursing graduate Sally Smith was awarded top honours in the NSW Health Excellence in Nursing and Midwifery Award for developing a workplace culture program that recognises ‘superhero qualities’ like passion and reliability in surgical ward colleagues – often stretched to the limit at work.

Keep scrolling!

Future Fellow explores a new frontier
of geochemistry

Professor Ed Burton was awarded a Future Fellowship worth more than $1 million to explore how a critical mineral and potentially carcinogenic toxin called antimony – present in plastics, textiles, electronic and computer equipment – could be leaking into our environment.

Keep scrolling!

Drone and AI identify sharks by species

Research by Dr Andrew Colefax continues to develop the capacity for drones to reliably identify different species of shark and assist lifeguards and authorities with decision making on the ground. It could be a game-changer in keeping our surfers and swimmers safer in the water

Keep scrolling!

We got a new VC

Professor Tyrone Carlin took over as Vice Chancellor of the University in September. Professor Carlin moved into the role after two years as Deputy Vice Chancellor (Academic) at Southern Cross, following a wide-ranging career with other institutions, including the University of Sydney.

Keep scrolling!

A Shruder way to tackle plastic pollution

The Plastic Collective, which includes Southern Cross, was awarded a $2.49 million dollar grant from the Australian Government to extend their work developing on-site Shruder Recycling Stations that can recycle plastic waste in remote and regional communities into materials that can be on-sold.

Keep scrolling!

A VR project made employment easier for people with disability

Our digital media students and staff created a portable Virtual Reality product together with Summerland Farm to help introduce people with disability to employment environments and tasks before they start work, making the transition to work that much easier.

Keep scrolling!

Money’s not everything but it sure helps when you study

Extra funding under the Destination Australia scholarship program, designed to encourage students to study in regional areas, boosted Southern Cross scholarships to record levels, with almost 400 scholarships worth $4.1million available.

Keep scrolling!

Investigating trees as producers of greenhouse gases

The Australian Research Council awarded a Southern Cross-led project more than $360,000 to explore the role trees play in the production cycle of the potent greenhouse gas methane. The ground-breaking project is being led by our very own Professor Damien Maher.

Keep scrolling!

Alumni in action during COVID-19

From bolstering Australia Post services to cope with lockdown shopping, to nursing on the frontlines, making hand sanitiser in a distillery and sourcing much-needed PPE for health services, our fantastic alumni made us more than proud this year.
