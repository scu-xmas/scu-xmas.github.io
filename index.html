While we navigated lockdowns, border closures and mastered online learning, the wheels at Southern Cross University didn’t stop turning. We bring you our top 12 moments from 2021, from ground-breaking research, to Lego water dragons and an Electric Kombi. Happy scrolling!

Our vintage Kombi got the upgrade of the century

We unveiled one of Australia’s most ambitious electric vehicle prototypes – a long-range converted Volkswagen Kombi. Converted right here on our Lismore campus, the project was a collaborative effort between the University, the NSW State Government, our students and lead contractor EV Machina.

We welcomed a new Chancellor

We welcomed one of Australia’s leading business figures, Sandra McPhee AM, as the new Chancellor for Southern Cross University. With a long and successful history across multiple industries in Australia, Ms McPhee was awarded the Member of the Order of Australia for significant service to business and to the community through leadership and advisory roles.

Our researchers weighed in on climate change

From microbes in trees eating methane for breakfast, to methane emissions from ‘blue carbon’ mangrove ecosystems, our research supported the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change Assessment Report 6, commissioned by the United Nations.

We got chatting

We launched our very own student-led podcast, the SCU Buzz. Each week our student hosts chatted with researchers, academics and students about anything from making sense of climate change, to lockdown protests, and the psychology of swearing.

Students teamed up with NSW Police to prevent cybercrime

A small team of law, accounting and IT students were crowned the joint winners of the inaugural NSW Police Cybercrime Think Tank, with an idea to prevent cybercrime that will be deployed by NSW Police.

Lego masterpieces were created

Five teams of students went head-to-head in a live Lego build event under the guidance of Lego Master superstars Andrew Tuppen and Damian Hinds. Each team used their creativity and design skills to create a 3D masterpiece depicting an aspect of the Gold Coast's landscape and cultural diversity, with a water dragon as the centrepiece.

We got behind a circular economy in the Northern Rivers

Southern Cross University spearheaded a new circular economy management initiative for the NSW Northern Rivers in partnership with industry that reduces waste while creating jobs and driving innovation. Headquartered at our Lismore campus, ReCirculator delivers four demonstration projects that apply circular economy principles in manufacturing, agriculture and food production.

Keep scrolling!

Speech pathology grew in Coffs Harbour

Our Coffs Harbour campus unveiled its new $500,000 Speech Pathology facilities, including a new Health Clinic due to open to the public next year. This is the first step towards the University’s vision of establishing a major health clinic at the Coffs Campus that accommodates occupational therapy, psychology, youth mental health, nursing, exercise science, injury rehabilitation, Indigenous health, midwifery and telehealth practices.

Keep scrolling!

Australian first Lifestyle Medicine degree heralded new era for education and care

In an exciting milestone for practitioners and preventative health, Southern Cross University launched a ground-breaking new Master of Lifestyle Medicine degree. As the only Master of Lifestyle Medicine degree currently offered in Australia, we’re set to welcome new students to the course in 2022.

Physical sciences hit the global top 3%

Southern Cross University skated up the Times Higher Education rankings for physical sciences, now placing in the top 3% of universities globally for the subject ranking. The physical sciences category encompasses geology, environmental, earth and marine sciences, as well as mathematics and statistics, physics and astronomy, and chemistry.

The Southern Cross Model premiered and expanded

Our revolutionary new teaching model was introduced as a pilot for a selection of courses in 2021. Student satisfaction and pass rates improved to such an extent that it will now be used to teach most of the University’s courses in 2022 and all courses by the end of 2023.

Keep scrolling!

We sea-lebrated the 40th anniversary of mass coral spawning discovery

Back in 1981 to bear witness and document the remarkable phenomenon of the Great Barrier Reef’s mass coral spawning was Distinguished Professor Peter Harrison, then a young PhD researcher. He’s devoted his life’s work to reef ecology and coral reproduction, developing and refining a novel coral larval restoration technique, now known as coral IVF.
